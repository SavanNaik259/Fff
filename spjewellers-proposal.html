Order Tracking Example Flow

Order Placement

When a customer places an order, they receive a confirmation email from the website with order details like:

Order ID
Items and quantity
Delivery address
Payment status, etc.

Order Processing (Warehouse or Seller Side)

The order is sent to the seller or warehouse, where they receive the same order details. In the admin panel, the order needs to be manually confirmed to update the status from "Processing" to "Confirmed".

Shipping Partner Integration Two Ways to Handle Tracking:

Option 1: Manual Status Update

Once the order is packed, the admin panel is updated to mark it as "Packed" after "confirm"

The package is then handed over to the delivery team.

The delivery team updates the order status in the admin panel as it moves:

  • Shipped
  • In Transit / At Specific Location
  • Out for Delivery
  • Delivered
Option 2: Barcode Scanning

Once packed, the system generates a barcode based on the customer's Order ID.

The delivery team or warehouse staff sticks this barcode label on the package.

As the delivery team scans the barcode at each step, the system automatically updates the order status:

  • Packed
  • Shipped
  • In Transit
  • Out for Delivery
  • Delivered

RAZORPAY PAYMENT INTEGRATION

Accept UPI, credit/debit card, net banking, paypal and other modes of payment

Razorpay Transaction Fee

Whenever a customer makes a successful payment, Razorpay deducts a small processing fee. Below is a breakdown:

Payment Method Razorpay Charges
UPI 2%
Credit/Debit Card 2%
Net Banking 2%
Wallets / EMI etc. 2% to 3%

Note: Razorpay also charges 18% GST on their fee (not on your sale amount).

Example:

If you sell jewellery worth 50,000 and the customer pays via credit card:

  • Razorpay Fee = 2% of 50,000 = 1,000
  • GST on Fee = 18% of 1,000 = 180
  • Total deduction = 1,180
  • Amount credited to your account = 50,000 1,180 = 48,820

If you are a GST-registered business, you can claim the 180 GST paid to Razorpay as Input Tax Credit (ITC) in your GST returns.

Razorpay Invoice

Every month, Razorpay gives you a PDF bill (called invoice) that shows:

  • How much fee they took from your payments
  • How much GST (18%) they charged on that fee

Example: product price = 50,0002%= 1,000 transaction fee + 180 GST = 1,180 total Razorpay deduction

What is GST Input Credit?

When you pay GST to someone (like Razorpay, courier companies, software services, etc.), you can inform the government through your GST return:

"I have already paid 180 GST to Razorpay, so reduce 180 from the GST I need to pay on my sales."

This is called Input Tax Credit (ITC).

Simple Example

  • You sell jewellery worth 1,00,000 this month.
  • You collect 3% GST from customers = 3,000
  • You need to pay this 3,000 to the government
  • But during the month, Razorpay deducted:
    • 1,000 as fees
    • 180 as GST on those fees
  • So while filing your GST return, you say: "Since I already paid 180 GST to Razorpay, I will reduce that from my payment and now pay only 2,820 to the government."
  • This is how GST Input Credit helps reduce your tax liability.
